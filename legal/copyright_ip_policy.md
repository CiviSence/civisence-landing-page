## 1. Introduction
CiviSence respects the intellectual property (IP) rights of all creators and expects its users to do the same. As a platform hosting user-generated civic reports, comments, and media files, we comply with intellectual property laws, including the Indian Copyright Act, 1957, and the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA). This policy outlines how we handle copyright infringement claims and user-uploaded IP.

## 2. User Responsibility and Licenses
* **Ownership:** When you upload images, videos, or comments, you represent that you own the content or possess the necessary rights and permissions to post it.
* **License Grant:** You retain ownership of your content. However, by uploading content, you grant CiviSence a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide, transferable, and sublicensable license to host, display, distribute, process, and modify the content solely for operating, promoting, and improving the Platform.

## 3. Reporting Copyright Infringement (Takedown Notice)
If you believe that any content hosted on CiviSence infringes your copyright or trademark rights, you may submit a takedown notification.

To be valid, your notice must be sent to our Grievance Officer at [civisence@zohomail.in](mailto:civisence@zohomail.in) or [civisence@gmail.com](mailto:civisence@gmail.com) and contain:
1. **Identification of the copyrighted work** claimed to have been infringed (e.g. details of the original photograph/video).
2. **Identification of the material** that is claimed to be infringing and its location (e.g. the specific URL, issue ID, or comment link on the platform).
3. **Your contact details:** Name, address, telephone number, and email address.
4. **Good Faith Statement:** A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
5. **Statement of Accuracy:** A statement that the information in the notification is accurate and, under penalty of perjury, that you are the owner or authorized to act on behalf of the owner of the copyright.
6. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature.

---

## 4. Counter-Notification Process
If your content was removed due to a copyright notice and you believe it was removed in error, you may file a counter-notification.

Send your counter-notice to [civisence@zohomail.in](mailto:civisence@zohomail.in) containing:
1. **Identification of the material** that was removed and its previous location.
2. **Consent to Jurisdiction:** A statement consenting to the jurisdiction of the competent courts in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India, and that you will accept service of process from the person who provided the original takedown notice.
3. **Statement under Penalty of Perjury:** A statement that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
4. **Signature:** Your physical or electronic signature.

Upon receiving a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to the original complaining party. If they do not file a legal action within ten (10) business days, we may restore the removed material.

---

## 5. Repeat Infringer Policy
CiviSence maintains a strict repeat infringer policy. Accounts that receive three (3) or more valid copyright takedown notices will be flagged for review, and their access may be suspended or permanently revoked (`is_banned = True`).
