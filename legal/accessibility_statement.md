# CiviSence Accessibility Statement

**Version:** 0.0.0.1  
**Effective Date:** July 8, 2026  
**Last Updated:** July 8, 2026  

---

## 1. Accessibility Commitment
CiviSence is built to help everyone—including students, local citizens, campus visitors, and municipal staff—collaborate on resolving civic issues. We are committed to ensuring digital accessibility for individuals with disabilities. We strive to continually improve the user experience of our website and mobile applications, applying relevant accessibility standards.

---

## 2. Conformance Status
We target conformance with the **Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA** standards. These guidelines outline how to make web content more accessible to people with wide ranges of disabilities, including visual, auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, and neurological limitations.

---

## 3. Implemented Accessibility Features
Our web dashboards (hosted on Vercel) and mobile applications incorporate the following accessibility features:
* **Semantic HTML:** We enforce HTML5 semantic elements to ensure screen readers and assistive technologies parse page structures accurately.
* **Keyboard Navigation:** Core user flows (submitting an issue, upvoting, commenting) are fully navigable via standard keyboard controls without requiring a mouse.
* **Contrast and Styling:** Text-to-background contrast ratios are designed to meet WCAG AA requirements, and styling remains responsive to text resizing settings.
* **Descriptive Alternative Text:** Image elements within the static UI include descriptive `alt` tags.

---

## 4. Known Limitations (User-Generated Content)
While we aim for full accessibility conformance, certain limitations exist concerning user-submitted content:
* **User Media:** Photographs and videos uploaded by users to document civic issues (e.g. photos of broken water pipes) may lack descriptive alternative text or video captions.
* **Map Pinpointing:** Our interactive mapping and geographic pinpointing controls may present navigation difficulties for screen reader users. In these instances, we provide standard text fields as an alternative to enter location addresses and building names.

---
